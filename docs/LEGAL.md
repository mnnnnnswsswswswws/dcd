# Legal & Responsible Use

iKali bundles tools that can probe, scan, and attack computer systems and
networks. Using them against systems you do not own or are not explicitly
authorized to test is **illegal** in most jurisdictions (e.g. the U.S. Computer
Fraud and Abuse Act, the UK Computer Misuse Act, Germany's §202a–c StGB, and
equivalents worldwide) and can carry serious criminal and civil penalties.

## You may use these tools only when at least one is true

- The system/network is **your own**.
- You have **explicit written permission** from the owner (a signed engagement,
  scope document, or rules of engagement).
- You are operating inside a **deliberately vulnerable lab** you control
  (e.g. a local VM, a CTF range, HackTheBox/TryHackMe machines you are entitled
  to attack).
- You are participating in an authorized **bug-bounty program**, strictly within
  its published scope and rules.

## You must never

- Scan, enumerate, or exploit third-party systems "just to see if it works."
- Exceed the agreed scope (IP ranges, hosts, time windows, techniques).
- Use these tools to access, exfiltrate, modify, or destroy data you are not
  authorized to touch.
- Intercept communications you are not a party to or authorized to monitor.

## Stay in scope

- Keep the signed authorization and scope handy for the duration of testing.
- Prefer the most targeted, least disruptive technique that achieves the goal.
- Avoid denial-of-service and destructive actions unless explicitly authorized.
- Log your activity; report findings responsibly and only to the owner.

## Wireless and capture notes

Wireless attack tooling and packet capture are included for **offline analysis
and education**. iOS does not give Linux (iSH or UTM) monitor-mode access to the
iPhone's radios, and live capture is limited. Do not attempt to intercept WiFi or
traffic you are not authorized to monitor.

If you are unsure whether an activity is authorized: **do not do it.**
